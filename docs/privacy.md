# Privacy

Scribe is built to keep your voice and your transcripts on your own Mac.

## Everything runs on your Mac

Audio never leaves your Mac, and no network connection is needed for
transcription. Scribe transcribes your speech on-device, using the Parakeet
speech model running on the Apple Neural Engine. Your recording is turned into
text right where it was captured.

## No cloud, no accounts

- There is no sign-up and no account. You download Scribe and use it.
- Your audio is not uploaded to any server for transcription.
- There is no telemetry.

## Your data stays yours

Recordings and transcripts are saved to a folder you choose, on your Mac. You
decide where they live, how long they are kept, and when they are deleted:

- You set the save location, and each recording mode can point somewhere of its
  own. See [Preferences reference](preferences.md#storage).
- Automatic cleanup is off unless you turn it on, and it only ever deletes audio
  files — never your transcripts. See
  [Preferences reference](preferences.md#storage-cleanup).
- You can remove all of Scribe's data at any time. See
  [Troubleshooting](troubleshooting.md#uninstall-or-reset-scribe).

## Permissions are used only for their feature

macOS asks you to approve each permission Scribe uses, and Scribe asks only for
what its features need — the microphone to hear you, Accessibility to paste at
your cursor, Screen Recording for Meeting mode, and (optionally) Notifications.
Nothing runs in the background beyond what those features do. Each one is
explained in [Getting started](getting-started.md#first-run-setup-wizard).
